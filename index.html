Tehran

Tehran, the bustling capital of Iran, is a vibrant metropolis nestled at the foot of the Alborz Mountains. Known for its rich history, Tehran blends ancient Persian culture with modernity, making it a unique destination. The city is home to important landmarks like the Golestan Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and the Milad Tower, one of the tallest structures in the world. Tehran's vibrant markets, such as the Grand Bazaar, offer a glimpse into local life, while its museums, including the National Museum of Iran, showcase the country's vast historical legacy. Despite its rapid urban growth, Tehran is also dotted with beautiful parks like Laleh and Mellat, providing residents and visitors with green spaces to relax. As the political, cultural, and economic heart of Iran, Tehran is a city of contrasts, where tradition and modernity coexist harmoniously.

Milad Tower, standing at 435 meters (1,427 feet), is the tallest tower in Iran and the sixth-tallest telecommunications tower in the world. Located in Tehran, it is a striking symbol of the city's modernization and technological advancement.

Tehran boasts a diverse and evolving transportation system that caters to its large population. The city is served by a comprehensive network of buses, including the Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) system, which provides efficient and affordable travel across key routes. The Tehran Metro, one of the largest subway systems in the Middle East, offers a reliable alternative to road traffic, connecting various districts and reducing congestion.
